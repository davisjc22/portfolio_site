CatHacks IV: Shader Development Environment

At CatHacks IV, my team and I developed a dynamic environment for creating and modifying graphical shaders while also being able to view the changes in real-time. Our vision for our environment was for developers to be able to create and view their work simultaneously to eliminate the process of making changes, compiling, viewing and repeating.

We also wanted to branch into the realm of virtual reality, so we made the shaders editable and viewable from an Oculus Rift, creating another medium of interaction between developers and their projects.

My team earned first place at CatHacks for our project and demo. We expanded on our efforts by attending Eastern Kentucky University's Student Symposium in Math and Computer Science to present our project. While there, we were presented with second place.
