First Place @ CatHacks for Shader Development Environment

At CatHacks IV, my team and I developed a dynamic environment for creating and modifying graphical shaders while also being able to view the changes in real-time. Shaders are commonly used in place of 3D rendering for their advantage of requiring less processing power. Our vision for our environment was for developers to be able to create and view their shaders simultaneously to eliminate the process of making changes, compiling, viewing and repeating. This resulted in us creating a side-by-side view of a text editor and a real-time rendering of the shader. We also wanted to branch into the realm of virtual reality shaders, so we created a feature that allows users to map variables in their script to buttons and sticks on Oculus Rift controllers, creating another medium of interaction between developers and their projects. My team earned first place at CatHacks for our project and demo. We expanded on our efforts by attending Eastern Kentucky University's Student Symposium in Math and Computer Science to present our project. While there, we were presented with second place.
